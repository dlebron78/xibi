# Scoring Rubric

10 weighted dimensions used to produce a 1.0–5.0 composite score. Weights are defaults; archetype overrides apply (see archetypes.md).

## Dimensions

| # | Dimension | Default Weight | Description |
|---|-----------|----------------|-------------|
| 1 | Role-skill alignment | 25% | How well the candidate's skills match the posting's stated requirements. Hard requirement misses (e.g., missing a mandatory language/framework) are penalized more heavily than soft misses. |
| 2 | Seniority match | 15% | Does the posting's expected experience level match the candidate's actual level? Overqualified is penalized less than underqualified. |
| 3 | Industry/domain fit | 10% | Domain experience match. If candidate has direct industry experience, full score. Adjacent experience scores 60–80%. No relevant domain experience: 30–50% unless the posting explicitly welcomes domain-changers. |
| 4 | Technical requirements coverage | 15% | Specifically for technical roles: tools, languages, platforms, methodologies listed as required. Score = (matched requirements / total requirements). |
| 5 | Cultural alignment signals | 8% | Inferred culture fit from posting language: pace ("move fast"), structure ("process-driven"), values ("mission-driven"), team dynamics. Compare against candidate's stated preferences. |
| 6 | Compensation alignment | 10% | Estimated compensation range vs candidate's target and minimum. No posted comp = neutral (5/10), not penalized. |
| 7 | Growth/trajectory fit | 7% | Does this role advance the candidate's stated career trajectory? A lateral move at higher comp may still score high. A step backward in scope scores low unless justified by other factors. |
| 8 | Location/remote policy | 5% | Match between posting's location/remote policy and candidate's work_preference. Remote-required candidate + onsite-required posting = 0/10. |
| 9 | Company stage match | 3% | Candidate's preference (startup/growth/enterprise) vs company's actual stage. Only penalizes when preference is strongly stated. |
| 10 | Career narrative coherence | 2% | Does this role fit cleanly in the candidate's professional story? A coherent next step scores high; a confusing detour scores low. |

## Composite Score Bands

| Score | Grade | Recommendation |
|-------|-------|----------------|
| 4.5–5.0 | Excellent | Apply immediately. Strong natural fit. |
| 3.5–4.4 | Good | Apply. Solid match with manageable gaps. |
| 3.0–3.4 | Worth Considering | Apply selectively. Real gaps exist — go in clear-eyed. |
| 2.0–2.9 | Weak | Not recommended. Significant mismatches that will hurt candidacy. |
| 1.0–1.9 | Poor | Skip. Fundamental incompatibility. |

**Rule:** Below 3.0, the evaluation must explicitly state why applying is not recommended.

## Persona Modifier Adjustments

Applied when candidate profile declares the modifier:

**recent_graduate** (< 2 years experience):
- Seniority match: weight reduced to 8%, penalizes less for junior positions
- Role-skill alignment: increased to 30% — raw skills matter more than experience
- Industry/domain fit: weight reduced to 5% — domain expected to be thin

**career_changer** (switching industries/domains):
- Industry/domain fit: weight reduced to 5%, adjacent experience treated as equivalent
- Role-skill alignment: increased to 30% — transferable skills are the argument
- Career narrative coherence: 0% — story discontinuity is expected and normal

**career_returner** (returning from gap ≥ 6 months):
- Seniority match: weight reduced to 10%
- Technical requirements coverage: recency penalty applied only if gap > 18 months AND technology has changed materially
- Growth/trajectory fit: weight increased to 12% — getting back on track matters

**international** (credentials from outside target market):
- Industry/domain fit: credentials evaluated for transferability, not direct match
- Compensation alignment: adjusted for purchasing power parity if compensation is in different currency

## Archetype Weight Overrides

Some archetypes shift dimension weights. See archetypes.md for per-archetype overrides.
The archetype's weight overrides replace (not add to) the defaults listed above.
